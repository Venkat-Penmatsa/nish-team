I'm new to Belgium - how do I get set up?
Most colleagues already know Belgium - this quick-start is for anyone newly arrived. HR can guide you: hr@nishtech.be.

In your first week
• Register at your local commune (gemeente / commune)
• Open a Belgian bank account
• Set up a phone / SIM
• Get your public-transport / bus pass (STIB/MIVB, De Lijn or TEC; national rail SNCB/NMBS)

In your first month
• Join a mutuality (health fund)
• Register with a GP - and a paediatrician & ONE if you have children
• Sort your rental contract & address registration

Also good to know
• Your national registration number, hospital insurance vs. mutuality, cost of living & where to stay
• Belgium has three languages (Dutch, French, German); English is widely used at work
• A welcoming, multilingual society with rich restaurant/café culture, festivals & local traditions

Can I work from home (telework)?
Structural telework is possible by agreement with NISH, in line with your individual arrangement and base schedule. NISH supports the equipment needed; you stay reachable during working hours and keep company data secure. It isn't an automatic right - if your presence is needed at the office or client on a planned home-working day, you attend. Arrange it via hr@nishtech.be.

Appraisals are held annually in 1-to-1 meetings - reviewing achievements, setting objectives, identifying training needs and supporting career development. Salary reviews and promotions may be considered as part of the process. → hr@nishtech.be.

Holidays & time off

How much annual holidays do I get?
20 Paid Holidays for a full-time, five-day week, plus 12 RTT/ADV days, plus Belgium's Public & National Holidays. Paid holidays are accrued on the previous year's work and credited in January; at NISH they should be used within the year. If you didn't work a full previous calendar year, paid holidays are pro-rated (about 5 days per quarter worked).
What are RTT / ADV days?
RTT/ADV days compensate for working beyond the standard 38-hour week (e.g. a 40-hour week for 20 working days in a month earns about one RTT day per month). They're calculated through the year; any unused RTT days are paid out with the December payslip and reset at year-end (no carry-forward).
How do I apply Leave?
Apply directly in NEPtune (“Apply Leaves”). Choose the type - Paid Holidays, RTT/ADV Days, Comp off or Forwarded days - your balance is shown for each. Add the start/end dates (or tick Half-day) and a comment, then submit.
• Sick day: leave it blank and add a comment; send your medical certificate to hr@nishtech.be.
• Client/customer holidays: apply in the order RTT → Comp off → Paid holidays.
• Request your main holiday ~1 month in advance and other days at least 1 week ahead. For special leave types, contact hr@nishtech.be.
Can I carry forward unused holidays for next year?
You may carry forward up to 5 unused paid holidays to the next calendar year; these must be used by 31 March or they lapse. RTT/ADV days cannot be carried forward (they're paid out at year-end).
How do Public holidays and comp-off work?
Belgium has 10 Public holidays. If one falls on a weekend, you receive a compensatory day (comp-off).
Why do I need to apply for leave on a client holiday?
Client holidays aren't automatically Belgian public holidays. If the client is closed but NISH is operational, apply for leave using your RTT days (or an arrangement approved by your manager). You can also use up to 5 training days a year during client holidays, with manager approval.
I have no holiday balance left - what are my options?
Options include unpaid leave, parental leave or European leave (subject to eligibility). Discuss with your manager and hr@nishtech.be before committing to travel.
How do I report sick leave?
Notify NISH (and your client manager) immediately by phone - not by SMS or e-mail - at the latest at your normal start time, and send a medical certificate to hr@nishtech.be within 24 hours. During the first 14 days stay reachable for a control doctor at your address, in principle 10:00-14:00 (you may go out for medical care). NISH pays guaranteed salary for the first period; for longer absence the mutuality takes over and HR initiates the process. Full rules are in the Work Regulations (English translation available).
What about parental or educational leave?
Parental leave lets eligible employees reduce or suspend work to care for a child - HR helps with eligibility, application and duration. Educational leave may apply for recognised training/studies (prior approval and documentation required) - contact HR before enrolling. → hr@nishtech.be.
Short leave for life events (marriage, birth, bereavement)?
Belgian “short leave” (klein verlet) gives paid time off for certain events, e.g. your marriage (3 days), birth of your child (20 days), death of your spouse/partner, child or a parent (3 days), and others. You may also take leave for compelling family reasons - generally up to 10 working days/year, unpaid. Full list & how to apply → hr@nishtech.be.

Compensation & Benefits

Payroll & payslip

When and how am I paid?
Salary is paid monthly by bank transfer, at the latest on the 5th working day of the month following the work performed. To be paid on time, keep your bank details on file and submit your timesheet by the 23rd.
My salary hasn't been credited - what should I do?
First check: your bank details are correct, you've received your payslip, and your timesheet was submitted. Belgian transfers can be delayed by weekends/holidays. If you still haven't been paid by the 5th of the following month, contact payroll@nishtech.be (or hr@nishtech.be).
Where can I see or download my payslip?
Payslips and payroll documents are in the SD Worx portal / mySDWorx app. You'll get a login email when you join, and a monthly email when each payslip is generated. SD Worx employee portal. Access issues → payroll@nishtech.be.
What does “13.92” mean?
Your annual package equals about 13.92 × your monthly salary: 12 monthly salaries + a 13th month (year-end bonus) + double holiday pay (~0.92 of a month). It's paid out across the year, with the two extra elements in July and December.
When is the year-end bonus (13th month) paid?
In December, one month's gross salary. At NISH this applies where your joining date is before 30 June; it's set via the applicable sector agreement / trade union / company policy (pro-rata for that first year).
When is double holiday pay paid?
Usually in July. It's a supplement of about 92% of your gross monthly salary, separate from your normal salary while on holiday. It's based on the days you worked in the previous calendar year (full or pro-rata by joining date).
What is indexation?
Wages under Joint Committee 200 are indexed annually on 1 January to reflect the cost of living, protecting your purchasing power. A performance-based increase may also apply.
Can I request a salary advance?
Salary advances are exceptional and need management approval - include the reason, amount and repayment expectation. If approved, you sign a repayment undertaking. Ask payroll@nishtech.be / hr@nishtech.be.

Vouchers & allowances

How do meal vouchers work?
Loaded onto your meal-voucher card for food and groceries - up to €8 per worked day
How do eco vouchers work?
For eco-friendly products and services from an official list. Up to €250 per year, valid 2 years. Issued via your benefits-card provider (credited to your card, typically with the July salary).
My meal/eco voucher card is lost or stolen - what do I do?
Block the card with your provider straight away (your balance is protected), then a replacement is ordered - you can do this yourself, directly:
• Monizze - block via the app or my.monizze.be, or call +32 (0)78 05 33 77 (Mon-Sat, 8:30-18:00). Website: monizze.be.
• Pluxee - block by calling 02 547 55 02 (24/7) or via the Pluxee BE app; a new card arrives in 5-10 working days. Website: pluxee.be.
Tip: register on your provider's app/portal so you can manage the card and get support directly. Not sure which provider you have? Check your card or ask hr@nishtech.be. Per the work regulations, the replacement cost (at most the value of one meal voucher, or €5 for an eco card) may be deducted from your next net pay.
What is the mobility allowance / mobility budget?
The Belgian mobility budget lets eligible employees choose sustainable alternatives to a company car - public transport, sustainable transport, housing near work, or a remaining cash balance. Your budget/category is based on your package and grade. More info: mobiliteitsbudget.be. To set it up → payroll@nishtech.be / fleet@nishtech.be.

Insurance & healthcare

What insurance does NISH provide, and what do I arrange myself?
NISH provides hospitalisation insurance through DKV Belgium (Premium Corporate plan) for eligible employees. You apply for your mutuality (health fund) and, for families, child benefits (e.g. KidsLife, Famiris, Parentia, MyFamily).
How do I declare or follow up a hospitalisation (DKV)?
Manage everything in MyDKV (mydkv.be) or the DKV app - declare a hospitalisation, submit medical costs and follow up reimbursements; payment can go directly to the hospital via the Medi-Card. Customer service: +32 (0)2 287 64 11. Hospitalised or in difficulty abroad? Call DKV Assistance +32 (0)2 230 31 32 and declare within 24 hours. Website: dkv.be. Policy questions → hr@nishtech.be.
What is mutuality, and is it mandatory?
Mutuality is Belgium's public health insurance. Joining a recognised fund is mandatory for anyone living or working in Belgium - you pay a small contribution and it reimburses most medical costs and gives access to public healthcare. Options include CAAMI/HZIV (free but basic), Solidaris, Mutualia or Partenamut.
Does NISH provide dental insurance?
No - NISH doesn't provide dental insurance. Check dental options with your mutuality.

Timesheets

How do I fill in my timesheet?
Record your actual hours each day in NEPtune, upload the client-approved timesheet (APTS) at month-end, and ensure manager approval where required. Timely submission drives payroll, client invoicing and project reporting.
When are timesheets due, and where?
Submit in the NEPtune portal by the 23rd of every month, then submit the client-approved timesheet by the 1st of the following month. You're notified by email before the freeze each month.
What is a frozen timesheet?
A frozen timesheet is locked after the monthly deadline. After freezing, corrections need HR Operations approval, and late submissions can delay payroll and client invoicing. → timesheets@nishtech.be.
I made a mistake or need a correction.
Email timesheets@nishtech.be with the period and the correction needed.
